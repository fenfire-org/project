A Step to Identities

What is identity? As speaking of identities you may think of your own identity. Now we are not speaking of human identity but rather an identity which is defined and represented in computer. However, human identity equates with virtual computer identity in many ways. Since - identity is unique .

Unique, matchless identity means that you can not find another yourself in the world. You can find people who look the same but they are not you exactly.

Above thinking raises questions like:

  • what is the agreed form to represent identity in computer?
  • how to avoid collisions to same identity in global?
  • we have a web and it uses unique identities already?

It's true that current web uses URIs, that is universal resourcen identifier. One has to said that web has certain weaknesses, for example weak permanence, locality and very small versioning. Pros are dynamic content and current use. What would be a better solution for web?`Of course one can always talk about better solutions. We will get back to some ideas later. In short better solutions do not represent a place, like current web does but the information itself.

Alternative choices are very easy to think. You can think whether a name would be a better solution. Simple antithesis invalidate this naming proposal since I know another people, other than myself, who has a name "Matti Katila".

Instead of names computer is good in numbers and therefore identity is better to represent as a one number. We can start from one or zero and continue with whole numbers but it would need an agreement who can have the one, who can have the two and so on. It is better to kill two flies with one attack and come up with a solution which doesn't need agreement to have addressing in used information space.

A system that doesn't use central contract to address identity is (cryptographic) hash function. Examples are MD5 and SHA-1 to say few. As a matter of thing Storm of Fenfire technologies is based on hash function to build a global information storage.

The heart of global identities in computing is deep in probability math and birthday problem . For example if assume that there is 10 billion people in worl and everyone of them uses 5 billion representation of identities in computer. The probability that two same people would get so bad lucky in lottery that in their computers different thing is represented with same identity is inordinate small.
